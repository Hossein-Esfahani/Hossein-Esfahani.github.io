AI/ML-Enabled Control Systems

Safe Learning • Control Theory • Networked Control • Model Predictive Control • Robotics • Autonomous Systems • Process Control

Welcome to my research website. My research focuses on the integration of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (including Reinforcement Learning, Bayesian Optimization, Gaussian Processes, and other data-driven methods) with optimization and control theory to develop AI/ML-enabled decision-making, estimation, and control algorithms for autonomous systems, Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs), Guidance, Navigation and Control (GNC), robotics, and Advanced Process Control (APC).

Research Interests

Model Predictive Control

Robust MPC, State Estimation-integrated MPC, Stochastic MPC, Data-Driven MPC and Distributed MPC.

Reinforcement Learning

Safe RL, MPC-based RL, and Multi-Agent RL.

Autonomous Systems

GNC, robotics, unmanned vehicles, and cyber-physical systems.

About Me

I received my M.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering from the University of Tabriz in 2012 and subsequently worked as an instrumentation and control engineer in the steel, power, and oil and gas industries, where I developed control, diagnostics, and monitoring systems for industrial automation. In 2018, I transitioned from industry back to academia, joining Gdańsk University of Technology (GUT) as a Research and Teaching Assistant before moving to the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) in 2020 as a Research Fellow. I earned my first Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering (Engineering Cybernetics) from NTNU in 2023, focusing on the integration of Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Model Predictive Control (MPC). I then joined Clemson University, where I completed a second Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering in 2025 and have since continued as a Postdoctoral Fellow. My research integrates artificial intelligence, machine learning, optimization, and control theory to develop AI/ML-enabled safety-critical control and decision-making algorithms for autonomous systems, cyber-physical systems, energy management, guidance, navigation and control (GNC), robotics, and advanced process control.
